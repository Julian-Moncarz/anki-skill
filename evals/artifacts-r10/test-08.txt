

TCP and UDP are both transport layer protocols, but they differ in key ways:

**TCP (Transmission Control Protocol)**
- **Connection-oriented**: establishes a connection via a 3-way handshake before sending data
- **Reliable**: guarantees delivery, ordering, and error-checking of packets
- **Flow/congestion control**: adjusts transmission rate to avoid overwhelming the receiver or network
- **Slower**: overhead from connection setup, acknowledgments, retransmissions
- **Use cases**: web browsing (HTTP), email, file transfer, SSH — anything where data integrity matters

**UDP (User Datagram Protocol)**
- **Connectionless**: just sends packets ("fire and forget")
- **Unreliable**: no guarantee of delivery, ordering, or duplicate protection
- **No flow control**: sends at whatever rate it wants
- **Faster**: minimal overhead, lower latency
- **Use cases**: video streaming, online gaming, DNS lookups, VoIP — where speed matters more than perfect delivery

**In short**: TCP trades speed for reliability; UDP trades reliability for speed. Choose based on whether your application can tolerate some data loss.
