

TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) and UDP (User Datagram Protocol) are both transport-layer protocols, but they differ in key ways:

**TCP** - Connection-oriented, reliable
- Establishes a connection via a 3-way handshake before sending data
- Guarantees delivery, ordering, and error checking
- Retransmits lost packets
- Flow control and congestion control built in
- Higher overhead/latency
- Used for: HTTP/HTTPS, email, file transfers, SSH

**UDP** - Connectionless, unreliable
- No handshake — just sends packets (datagrams)
- No delivery guarantee, no ordering, no retransmission
- Minimal overhead, lower latency
- Used for: DNS lookups, video streaming, gaming, VoIP

**The tradeoff**: TCP gives you reliability at the cost of speed; UDP gives you speed at the cost of reliability. Choose based on whether your application can tolerate packet loss.
